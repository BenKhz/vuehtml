What is Software Asset Management & How to Get Started

Software Asset Management (SAM) is a strategic approach to managing software resources across their lifecycle, encompassing tasks like procurement, license management, and compliance verification. By implementing SAM practices, organizations can achieve better visibility into their software inventory, ensure compliance, and optimize costs.

The Drivers of Software Asset Management

The drivers of Software Asset Management (SAM) arise from the complexity of software usage, changing licensing models, cost control, risk mitigation, and the alignment of software with business goals, prompting organizations to adopt SAM practices.

The SAM prime directive: Know your computing environment

The SAM Prime Directive "Know Your Computing Environment" underscores the fundamental principle of Software Asset Management (SAM). This directive emphasizes the importance of gaining a comprehensive understanding of an organization's software landscape, including software applications, licenses, usage patterns, and dependencies. By knowing the computing environment thoroughly, organizations can effectively manage software resources, ensure compliance, optimize costs, and make informed decisions about software procurement, deployment, and retirement. This directive serves as the cornerstone of successful SAM implementation, enabling organizations to maintain control over their software assets and maximize the value derived from them.
