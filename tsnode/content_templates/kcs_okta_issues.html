TROUBLESHOOT OKTA SIGN-IN ISSUES

What do I do if I can't sign in?

Occasionally, you may find yourself in a situation where you're having trouble logging into Okta. This could be for any number of reasons such as needing further authentication, incorrect username or password, or device conflict. In this article, we'll help you troubleshoot the top three issues and get to the root of the issue.

01

PROBLEM

I'm unable to sign in to Okta on my new phone.

All the apps on my work PC are working with two factor authentication through Okta Verify on my phone. I am also able to run apps in the same way on my personal computer, which is a MacBook. However, on my phone, when I open an application and am prompted for my credentials, Username and Password (Powered by Okta), I am met with the error 'Unable to sign in'.

SOLUTION

Okta works by binding to your hardware.

Okta Verify works by binding to your physical phone hardware and the installed software instance.

Okta Verify only works on one device for each Okta account. Uninstalling Okta Verify, Changing Phones, Transferring or Restoring your Phone Data, using Mobile Phone Cleaning Apps, or Resetting your Phone are some of the things that may result in the binding being broken between your phone and Okta Verify, and you will need to re-enroll your device in Okta Verify. To do this, you'll need to contact your organization's IT department.

However, you may be able to reset Okta Verify yourself. Check out https://support.okta.com/help/s/article/Resetting-your-Okta-Verify-Token?language=en_US. If you're unable to follow the steps in this document, or the option to select another factor isn't available, then please contact your IT Department and they will be able to assist you and get you up and running.

02

PROBLEM

I'm getting various login errors.

I'm getting the login error "Login Error: Could not validate the following SAML Authnrequest from the partner" and I can't seem to get around it. I'm also seeing "Login Error: Received an assertion that is valid in the future".

What do I do?

SOLUTION

You'll need to make some changes in settings and resync.

To fix the error "Login Error: Could not validate the following SAML Authnrequest from the partner":

1. Log in to the Access Gateway Admin UI console.

2.Select the Settings tab.

3.Select Advanced.

4.Verify that the time is correct.

5.If the time is not correct, click Resync.

6.Click the refresh button to refresh system time and verify that it is current. 

7.Test the application to determine if time is synchronized correctly..

To fix the error "Login Error: Received an assertion that is valid in the future":

1. Log in to the Access Gateway Management console.

2.Select the Service option, and then select the NTP option.

3.Choose the option to restart the NTP service.

4.Check the time in the Access Gateway web console.

5.Test the application.

03

PROBLEM

Browser doesn't trust the domain and doesn't post SAML request.

This error can occur if a user keeps reposting SAML assertions or if an error occurs in the process of accepting a self-signed SSL certificate.
Caused by: Exception: Unable to find the current binding.

SOLUTION

Try validation.

Validation and mitigation steps 1:

1.Open the returned certificate.

2.Verify the validity of the certificate.

3.If the certificate isn't valid, request a new certificate and update it in Access Gateway Management console.

4.Test the application.

Validation and mitigation steps 2:

1.Open the returned certificate.

2.Verify the validity of the certificate.

3.If the certificate isn't valid, request a new certificate and update it in Access Gateway Management console.

4.Test the application.
